# Deep Clean Tech: Viable Climate Companies by Tipping-Point Intervention Category

## TL;DR
- The strongest "biggest impact × genuinely viable" picks cluster in three tiers: (1) commercially bankable clean-firm-power and industrial-materials plays (Fervo Energy, Sublime Systems, Boston Metal); (2) carbon-removal categories with real delivered tonnes and offtake demand (biochar leader Exomad Green, enhanced-rock-weathering leaders Terradot/Lithos/Mati, and DAC leaders Climeworks/1PointFive/Heirloom); and (3) high-leverage but still pre-commercial frontier bets (methane removal, ocean CDR, ice intervention, microbial CO2 conversion) that are underfunded precisely because they are earlier.
- Methane removal is the highest-impact, least-funded category: open-atmosphere iron-salt-aerosol work is still pre-commercial research (Spark Climate Solutions, a nonprofit, has deployed >$8M across >27 grants), while point-source players like Windfall Bio (~$37M raised) and Denmark's Ambient Carbon (grant-funded, no disclosed venture round) show early delivered pilots.
- Funding figures move fast and are often self-reported; this report flags "announced/contracted" vs "delivered/verified" throughout and explicitly marks categories that are data-poor or pre-commercial rather than overstating them.

## Key Findings
- **DAC is comparatively well-funded and concentrated**: per CDR.fyi's 2025 DAC Market Snapshot, the top three suppliers (1PointFive, Climeworks, Heirloom) account for over 80% of total DAC tonnes contracted 2020–H1 2025, with 1PointFive at ~1.3M tonnes (52% of credits sold) and Climeworks leading deliveries (~81%). Yet delivered tonnage is tiny — only ~1,186 tonnes delivered by six DAC suppliers since 2023 (~0.05% of all contracted volume) — and costs remain ~$600–1,000/t, far from the ~$100/t target.
- **Enhanced rock weathering and biochar are the CDR categories with the most credible near-term scale-up**: they use existing agricultural/industrial infrastructure, have delivered verified tonnes, and attract large corporate offtakes (Microsoft, Google, Frontier).
- **Ocean CDR (OAE + direct ocean capture) is real but early**: median company funding is modest; Ebb Carbon's Microsoft deal (up to 350,000 tonnes over 10 years, Oct 2024) is the largest marine CDR commitment to date, but its initial delivery was just 1,333 tonnes.
- **Clean firm power is the clearest commercial winner**: Fervo Energy raised ~$2.2B gross in its May 2026 Nasdaq IPO and closed the first-ever non-recourse project financing for an enhanced-geothermal project (~$421M) — a genuine bankability milestone for a frontier climate technology.
- **Low-carbon cement/steel face policy risk**: Sublime Systems and Brimstone both lost DOE grants in 2025 but retain major private offtakes (Microsoft, Amazon).
- **The frontier categories (methane removal, ice intervention, microbial CO2 conversion) are genuinely underfunded and pre-commercial** — this is where the user's "underfunded technologies" thesis is most accurate, and where claims must be most carefully hedged.

## Details

### 1. Atmospheric / Point-Source Methane Removal & Mitigation
**Status: Highest-impact, least-funded, largely pre-commercial. Treat with caution.**

Per the IEA Global Methane Tracker, methane is responsible for nearly 30% of the rise in global average temperatures since the Industrial Revolution, and the IPCC gives it a 20-year global warming potential of ~84–87x that of CO2 — making near-term methane action one of the highest-leverage climate interventions. But the removal side is scientifically immature.

- **Spark Climate Solutions** (US; nonprofit, founded 2022) — Not a company but the central funder/convener of atmospheric methane removal research. Confirmed 501(c)(3); its methane-removal program has supported more than 27 research grants with more than $8 million in funding. Grantees include University of Copenhagen, CSIC (Spain), Utrecht, Cornell, and MIT (Jesse Kroll & Arlene Fiore, for chlorine-mediated methane chemistry). Useful as a map of who's credible in the space.
- **Ambient Carbon** (Denmark; spin-out of University of Copenhagen; founder Prof. Matthew Johnson) — "MEPS" (Methane Eradication Photochemical System): UV + chlorine photochemistry to destroy dilute methane at point sources (barns, biogas, wastewater). **No disclosed venture/equity funding** — grant-funded only (Innovation Fund Denmark via AgriFoodTure/PERMA project; partners include Arla Foods). **Delivered pilot**: a 40-ft container system treated ventilation air from a 250-cow dairy barn at Hofmansgave Foundation farm, removing up to 90% of methane (2025). **Announced (not delivered)**: a Danone-funded ~30x scale-up planned for a 4,000+ head dairy in Indiana, US. Credible science, very early commercially.
- **Windfall Bio** (US, San Mateo; founded 2022; CEO Josh Silverman) — Uses methane-eating microbes ("mems") to convert methane from any source into organic fertilizer + carbon credits. **~$37M total** ($9M seed March 2023 + $28M Series A April 2024, led by Prelude Ventures with Amazon Climate Pledge Fund, Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Mayfield). Targets agriculture, oil & gas, waste, and coal-mine methane (both concentrated CMM and dilute ventilation-air methane at 0.2–1%). Pilot-stage but the best-funded point-source methane play. Named TIME Best Invention 2024.
- **Iron-salt-aerosol (open atmosphere) for-profits — flag as speculative/pre-commercial**: **Bennu** (formerly Blue Dot Change; US; CEO David Henkel-Wallace) — no disclosed funding; pivoted from ocean iron-salt spraying to smaller ship-based UV methane destruction, with an at-sea pilot on a Lomar Shipping vessel (2024–25) pursuing Gold Standard certification. **Atmospheric Methane Removal AG (AMR)** (Switzerland; founded 2021) — shareholder-funded plus an undisclosed loan; voted to become non-profit-oriented in October 2024; nothing delivered in the field. Scientists warn commercialization is "way beyond what the science yet supports."
- **Enteric methane (agriculture) — more commercially mature than removal**: **Rumin8** (Australia; founded 2021; ~$30.8M raised, backers include Breakthrough Energy Ventures, Andrew Forrest) — synthetic anti-methanogenic compounds; trials show up to ~86–95% methane reduction; regulatory approval secured in Brazil. **Number 8 Bio** (Australia; founded 2022; ~A$20M cumulative across pre-seed/seed/Series A) — synthetic-biology feed additive + slow-release bolus. **Symbrosia** (US/Hawaii; Asparagopsis seaweed feed; backed by Danone Manifesto Ventures). **DSM-firmenich Bovaer (3-NOP)** — the incumbent, first EU-approved additive, ~30% enteric methane reduction.

**Caveat**: Open-atmosphere methane removal is TRL 1–3, contested, and carries governance/side-effect risks (chlorine toxicity, ozone impacts). Point-source and enteric approaches are more grounded.

### 2. Direct Ocean Carbon Capture & Ocean Alkalinity Enhancement
**Status: Real, scientifically credible, but early; modest funding; delivered volumes small.**

- **Ebb Carbon** (US; founded 2021; co-founders incl. Matt Eisaman) — Electrochemical OAE splitting seawater into acid/base streams. Raised a $20M+ Series A. Signed an agreement to remove up to 350,000 tonnes of CO2 over 10 years with Microsoft (Oct 2024), with an initial delivery of 1,333 tonnes — described as the largest marine CDR commitment to date. Pilot ("Project Macoma," Port Angeles) began operations 2025; 100-tonne/yr system at PNNL Sequim. Strongest commercial signal in ocean CDR.
- **Captura** (US, Pasadena; founded 2021; Caltech spin-out; CEO Steve Oldham) — Direct ocean capture via bipolar-membrane electrodialysis. ~$45M+ raised (Series A expanded to $45.3M; first close of a $12.5M Series B in June 2026 led by Equinor Ventures). Pilot plants in LA and Hawaii (1,000-tonne capacity). Backers include Equinor, Aramco Ventures, Eni Next, Maersk Growth.
- **Equatic** (US; UCLA spin-out) — Combines seawater electrolysis with marine mineralization; co-produces green hydrogen. Equatic-1 demonstration in Singapore (~3,650 t/yr capacity); larger plant announced. Pre-purchase agreement with Boeing.
- **Planetary Technologies** (Canada, Nova Scotia; founded 2019) — Magnesium-hydroxide OAE. ~$14.6M raised. **Delivered first net OAE credits to Shopify and Stripe** (138 tonnes, Nov 2024); $31M Frontier-facilitated offtake. Won a $1M XPRIZE XFACTOR award (2025).
- **Vesta** (US) — Coastal olivine spreading (enhanced coastal weathering); first US federal permit for a standalone ocean CDR pilot (North Carolina, 2024). Firmly pre-commercial.

**Caveat**: Ocean scientists have called for caution/halts on some open-ocean projects; MRV and ecological effects are unresolved. Cost targets (<$100/t by ~2028) are projections.

### 3. Enhanced Rock Weathering
**Status: One of the most credible near-term CDR categories; real delivered tonnes; large offtakes.**

- **Terradot** (US; founded 2022 at Stanford; CEO James Kanoff) — ERW on Brazilian farmland. Launched Dec 2024 with **$58.2M** funding (Google, Microsoft Climate Innovation Fund, John Doerr) and ~300,000 tonnes of contracted removals — including a 90,000-tonne/$27M Frontier purchase and Google's 200,000-tonne deal (its largest single CDR purchase). Acquired Eion (Feb 2026) to consolidate. Spread 48,000+ tonnes of rock over 1,800 hectares.
- **Lithos Carbon** (US; founded 2022; founders Noah Planavsky, Chris Reinhard, Mary Yap) — Basalt on US farmland with soil-sampling MRV. ~$63.4M raised (Greylock, Union Square Ventures, Bain Capital Ventures). 11,400-tonne Microsoft deal; delivered 5,160 registry-certified tonnes in Dec 2025 (described as the largest ERW issuance to date).
- **Mati Carbon** (US/India; founded 2022; CEO Shantanu Agarwal) — Basalt on smallholder farms in India/Africa; **won the $50M XPRIZE Carbon Removal grand prize (April 2025)**. Nonprofit-controlled (Swaniti Initiative). Tech-heavy MRV; buyers include Stripe, Shopify, Siemens. Strong impact + equity story.
- **UNDO** (UK/Canada) — Basalt/olivine ERW; Microsoft 28,900-tonne deal (first CDR deal structured with debt capital); $5M XPRIZE runner-up.
- **InPlanet** (Brazil/Germany) — ERW in tropical Brazil; signed a Microsoft ERW deal (Dec 2025).

**Caveat**: CarbonPlan notes experimental CO2-removal estimates vary by four orders of magnitude; MRV is improving but not settled.

### 4. Blue Carbon & Peatland / Ecosystem Restoration (with tech/MRV edge)
**Status: Scientifically robust for mangroves; MRV is the bottleneck and the tech opportunity. Mostly project-based, not venture-scale companies.**

- The blue carbon market is tiny (<1% of voluntary carbon credits) and mangrove-dominated (~99% of claimed removals). As of October 2025, ~81 projects existed but only ~10 were actively issuing credits. Premiums are high (S&P Global blue carbon assessment hit a record $29.30/tCO2e in August 2025) precisely because rigorous MRV is scarce and costly.
- **MRV/data players** are the tech edge: **Sylvera** (UK; carbon-credit ratings, building satellite-based peatland MRV with Innovate UK funding), and registries/standards (Verra VCS VM0033 for tidal wetlands/seagrass). Kelp/macroalgae remains methodologically uncreditable (permanence unproven).
- **Running Tide** (US) — Pioneered kelp-sinking + alkalinity buoys, backed early by Shopify; a cautionary tale — the company wound down its ocean CDR operations, underscoring the category's fragility.
- **Peatland**: Largely grant + carbon-finance driven (UK IUCN Peatland Code — ~361 projects/52,000 ha as of mid-2025; German MoorFutures). **aeco** (Germany) is an early venture rewetting European agricultural peatlands with continuous water-level MRV. Microsoft backed Pantheon Regeneration on US peatland restoration.

**Caveat**: These are high-integrity *avoidance* (not durable removal) in most cases; permanence is measured in decades with long monitoring tails.

### 5. Direct Air Capture
**Status: Comparatively well-funded; technology proven; cost and scale remain the barriers.**

- **Climeworks** (Switzerland; founded 2009) — Solid-sorbent DAC. Climeworks and 1PointFive together represent over $1.4B (60%) of the ~$2.3B of private DAC investment tracked 2021–H1 2025; Climeworks raised a $162M late-stage round in Q3 2025. Operates Orca and Mammoth (Iceland; Mammoth design capacity 36,000 t/yr). Leads in tonnes delivered (~81% of all DAC delivered) but actual delivered volumes are small and costs remain near ~$1,000/t; laid off ~10%+ of staff in 2025.
- **1PointFive / Carbon Engineering** (US; Occidental subsidiary) — Liquid-solvent DAC. Building Stratos (West Texas), designed for 500,000 t/yr — by far the largest. Leads DAC credits *sold* (~52%); Microsoft and Airbus offtakes. Note fossil-industry ownership and potential enhanced-oil-recovery use.
- **Heirloom Carbon** (US; founded 2020; CEO Shashank Samala) — Accelerated limestone mineralization DAC. Raised **$150M Series B** (Dec 2024; co-led by Future Positive and Lowercarbon Capital), bringing total to >$200M. Two Louisiana plants planned (~320,000 t/yr combined). Targets sub-$100/t at scale. Microsoft (up to 315,000 t) offtake.
- **Emerging/novel**: Phlair (electrochemical DAC, Germany), Mission Zero (UK), Carbyon (Netherlands), Skytree (Netherlands), Capture6, Avnos (hybrid DAC), Octavia Carbon (Kenya — the only Global South DAC supplier with contracted credits).

**Caveat**: US DOE terminated initial ~$50M tranches for Project Cypress (Climeworks/Heirloom) and the South Texas hub (1PointFive) in Oct 2025, though the administration later preserved some DAC funding in 2026 — policy risk is significant. Costs remain ~10x the target.

### 6. Biochar & Durable Bio-Based Carbon Removal
**Status: The most-delivered durable CDR category by volume; strong offtake demand.**

- **Exomad Green** (Bolivia; founded 2021) — World's largest biochar producer; converts forestry residues to biochar via pyrolysis. **Delivered over 320,000 tonnes** cumulatively; **Microsoft signed the world's largest biochar deal — at least 1.24M tonnes over 10 years (May 2025)**, described as the largest biochar carbon-removal deal in history by volume. Note a data conflict on annual run-rate: Exomad self-reports ~260,000 t/yr from two facilities (targeting 1M t/yr by 2027 across five sites), while S&P Global cites ~120,000 mtCO2e/yr currently — flag as unresolved. ~84% carbon content, >1,000-year stability; certified under Puro.earth.
- **Charm Industrial** (US; founded 2018) — Bio-oil injection into wells + biochar. Raised ~$130M. Google deals (100,000 tonnes biochar, 2025).
- **Varaha** (India) — Converts invasive Prosopis juliflora to biochar; Google offtake; digital MRV.
- **NetZero** (France) — Biochar from ag waste in Brazil/Cameroon; $15M XPRIZE runner-up.
- **Vaulted Deep** (US) — Injects organic waste slurry into geologic storage; $8M XPRIZE runner-up.
- **Carbo Culture / Carboneers / Novocarbo** (Europe) — Additional credible operators.

**Caveat**: Biochar permanence depends on production quality; MRV protocols (Puro, Isometric) are maturing.

### 7. Microbial / Bioelectrochemical & CO2/Methane-to-Products
**Status: Gas fermentation is commercial; microbial electrosynthesis and direct electro-CO2 conversion are still lab/pilot. Be honest about maturity — this is the user's own field.**

- **LanzaTech** (US, Skokie; founded 2005; CEO Jennifer Holmgren) — Gas fermentation converting industrial CO/CO2 to ethanol and chemicals. Six commercial facilities; produced 30M+ gallons of ethanol since 2021. Went public (SPAC) Feb 2023 at ~$2B valuation; spun out LanzaJet (SAF) and LanzaX (chemicals). Partners: ArcelorMittal, Lululemon, Zara, Coty. **The clear commercial leader** — but faces profitability pressure and grant-dependency risk.
- **Microbial electrosynthesis (MES)** — CO2 + electricity to carboxylic acids via biofilms on cathodes. Per peer-reviewed literature (Trends in Biotechnology, 2024), MES is at **TRL 3/4** — lab scale, needs process optimization before an industrial prototype. Directly relevant to the user's MESSAI work; honestly still pre-commercial.
- **Electro-CO2 conversion**: Dioxide Materials (CO2 electrolysis, partnered with LanzaTech via ARPA-E), Twelve (US; CO2-to-products/SAF), and others are scaling electrolyzer-based CO2 conversion — early commercial.

**Caveat**: Gas fermentation abates/recycles emissions but is not permanent CDR unless paired with sequestration. MES and electro-conversion economics remain unproven at scale.

### 8. Ice-Sheet / Arctic Intervention & Frontier Geoengineering
**Status: Very early, contested, tiny funding, mostly nonprofit/grant. Do NOT overstate.**

- **Real Ice** (UK; launched 2022) — Sea-ice thickening via underwater pumps/drones. Directors committed ~$5M; part of a research group that received the largest grant (~$13M) in the UK's ~$75M geoengineering program (2025); field trials in Cambridge Bay, Nunavut. Aspiration: refreeze ~1M km². Envisions "cooling credits."
- **Arctic Reflections** (Netherlands) — Sea-ice thickening via pumping platforms; raised ~$1.1M; trials in Svalbard and Newfoundland.
- **Ocean Visions** (US nonprofit) — Runs the Arctic Sea Ice research fund/road maps.

**Caveat**: In Oct 2024 a preprint by 42 glaciologists condemned ice-thickening and polar geoengineering as infeasible and dangerous; a Sept 2025 study concluded high-profile interventions are not viable and may cause harm, each costing ≥$10B. This is a "break-glass research" area, not an investable commercial category.

### 9. Wildcard High-Impact Adjacencies

**Clean firm power / geothermal — the strongest commercial category overall:**
- **Fervo Energy** (US, Houston; founded 2017; CEO Tim Latimer) — Enhanced geothermal (horizontal drilling + fiber-optic sensing). Raised ~$1.5B in private capital pre-IPO (incl. $462M Series E, Dec 2025, led by B Capital with Google), then **IPO'd on Nasdaq (FRVO) on May 14, 2026, raising ~$2.2B gross at $27.00/share**, and closed **~$421M in non-recourse project debt for Cape Station Phase I** (led by Barclays, BBVA, HSBC, MUFG, Société Générale) — the first non-recourse project financing for an enhanced-geothermal project globally, a landmark bankability milestone. Cape Station (Utah): first 100 MW in 2026, scaling to 500 MW by 2028, permitted to 2 GW. Fully contracted PPAs (Southern California Edison, Shell Energy). **Top-tier pick.**

**Low-carbon cement/steel:**
- **Sublime Systems** (US; founded 2020; MIT spin-out; CEO Leah Ellis) — Electrochemical "true-zero" cement. Raised >$200M (incl. Holcim, CRH, Siam Cement). Microsoft offtake (up to 622,500 tonnes). **DOE rescinded its $87M grant in 2025**, pausing the Holyoke plant and forcing a 10% layoff — strong tech, real policy risk.
- **Brimstone** (US; Oakland) — Calcium-silicate-rock cement (also yields alumina/critical minerals). Amazon offtake. Also lost a $189M DOE award (2025); raising private capital for a $378M demo plant.
- **Boston Metal** (US, Woburn; founded 2013) — Molten Oxide Electrolysis for zero-carbon steel + critical metals. **>$500M total raised** (incl. $75M in 2026 with Tata Steel). Produced 1+ ton of steel/iron at pilot scale (2025); commercial plant in Brazil focused on critical metals. Backed by Breakthrough Energy Ventures, ArcelorMittal.

**Other adjacencies**: long-duration energy storage (Form Energy — iron-air), industrial heat (Antora, Rondo — thermal batteries), fusion (Commonwealth Fusion Systems), and methane-slashing food/ag (covered in §1).

## Recommendations

**For a "companies to watch / invest in" layer, stage the shortlist by conviction:**

**Tier 1 — Biggest impact × proven commercial viability (feature prominently):**
1. **Fervo Energy** (clean firm power) — the clearest bankability proof-point; IPO + first-of-kind project debt.
2. **Exomad Green** (biochar) — most-delivered durable CDR by volume; landmark Microsoft deal.
3. **Terradot** and **Lithos Carbon** (ERW) — infrastructure-light, verified tonnes, blue-chip offtakes.
4. **Climeworks / 1PointFive / Heirloom** (DAC) — the well-funded leaders; frame honestly on cost/scale.
5. **LanzaTech** (gas fermentation) — the commercial leader in carbon-to-products.
6. **Sublime Systems** and **Boston Metal** (materials) — huge addressable emissions; flag policy risk.

**Tier 2 — High impact, credible, still scaling (watch closely):**
7. **Ebb Carbon** and **Captura** (ocean CDR) — best commercial signals in a promising-but-early category.
8. **Mati Carbon** (ERW) — best impact-per-dollar + equity story; XPRIZE validation.
9. **Windfall Bio** (point-source methane) — best-funded methane play with delivered pilots.
10. **Rumin8** (enteric methane) — furthest along in a high-impact agricultural niche.

**Tier 3 — Frontier / underfunded / pre-commercial (present as "high-leverage but early," with explicit caveats):**
11. **Ambient Carbon** (point-source methane destruction) — credible science, no venture round yet.
12. **Planetary Technologies** (OAE) — delivered first credits; watch MRV maturation.
13. **Real Ice / Arctic Reflections** (ice intervention) — label clearly as contested research, not investable.
14. **Microbial electrosynthesis** field (the user's own domain) — TRL 3/4; frame as a research frontier.

**Benchmarks that would change these rankings:**
- Methane removal moves up if any open-atmosphere approach passes a credible, governed field trial with net-climate-benefit verification.
- Ocean CDR moves up when a company delivers (not just contracts) >100,000 tonnes with settled MRV.
- DAC moves up sharply if any player demonstrably hits ~$100/t at commercial scale.
- Cement/steel de-risk if DOE awards are restored or private offtakes cover the financing gap.
- MES/electro-CO2 moves up on a first commercially operating prototype with a validated techno-economic case.

## Caveats
- **Funding figures move quickly and are often self-reported.** Totals here are drawn from company announcements, Crunchbase/PitchBook/Tracxn, CDR.fyi, and reputable press; cross-check before publishing any specific number, especially rounds after mid-2026. Note the unresolved conflict on Exomad Green's annual run-rate (company ~260,000 t/yr vs S&P ~120,000 mtCO2e/yr).
- **"Announced/contracted" ≠ "delivered/verified."** Most CDR offtakes (Microsoft, Google, Frontier) are forward purchases; delivered tonnes are far smaller than contracted volumes across every category (e.g., DAC has delivered only ~1,186 tonnes total since 2023 against millions contracted).
- **Policy risk is acute in the US** (DOE grant terminations in 2025 hit DAC and cement; some funding later preserved). Non-US and privately financed players are comparatively insulated.
- **Pre-commercial/data-poor categories are explicitly flagged**: methane removal (esp. iron-salt-aerosol), ice/Arctic intervention, and microbial CO2 conversion. These are the most underfunded — consistent with the user's thesis — but claims about them should remain hedged and clearly labeled as early-stage research.
- **Nonprofits and prizes matter here**: Spark Climate Solutions (methane), Ocean Visions (ice), Frontier/Milkywire (offtake), and XPRIZE shape which technologies advance — worth including alongside for-profits in the site.
